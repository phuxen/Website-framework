Who the hell is Paul Huxen?

Why, he's a chap that lives over in East London, has years worth of experience as a graphic designer and smells nice.

I'm Paul, I make nice things…

Always have done ever since I broke out a box of crayons and drew all over the walls. Thankfully now I don't draw on walls, I design tshirt collections for fashion labels or throw out a typeface through my font foundry – Kraken. Sometimes I can be found creating a UI for a website using HTML5, CSS3 and {LESS}, or knocking up some advertising and web banners in the Adobe CS5 suite.

Bollard

Typeface – 2010

Bollard is a fat and cuddly sans serif typeface which was created during typographic experiments with simple shapes and elements. I had seen many fat fonts however these were still all quite angular and didn't have the softer appearance that I craved. So I took it upon myself to construct this font made of circles and gentle curved corners and unleashed it into the wild a few months later.

Let The Kids Dance

Tshirt designs – 2009

Let The Kids Dance were a fashion company that took new graduates and talent from the worlds of design, art and fashion. My designs were to be used as part of the first women's clothing collection. The designs include previous work such as the typefaces Retro Disco and Fat Marker along with a new layout design for playing cards.
