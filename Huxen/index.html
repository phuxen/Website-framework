Bollard Typeface

Bollard is a fat and cuddly sans serif typeface which was created during typographic experiments with simple shapes and elements. I had seen many fat fonts however these were still all quite angular and didn't have the softer appearance that I craved. So I took it upon myself to construct this font made of circles and gentle curved corners and unleashed it into the wild a few months later.

Squiggles Typeface

A typeface that saw the end of my university life, it was shown in three exhibitions, My final degree show at University, Eleven Pt, an exhibition of eleven graduates which took place in Shoreditch, London & D&AD New Blood 2008. The poster that was displayed at New Blood was featured in the creative review blog.

Unremitting Typeface

When I was in the process of creating the squiggles typeface, this was an early prototype for my experimentations creating joined up letterforms. It has a series of connecting rods that reach up or down to the line above or below, thus enabling to connect multiple lines of text.

Let The Kids Dance Fashion brand

Let The Kids Dance were a fashion company that take new graduates and talent from the worlds of design, art and fashion. My designs were to be used as part of the first women's clothing collection. The designs include previous work such as the typefaces Retro Disco and Fat Marker along with a new layout design for playing cards. New artwork was created especially for the collection such as the hand drawn slogan “…and so we cannot know ourselves…” and the Paisley Monster.

Mr Site Character Illustration

The existing Mr Site character has been in use since the beginning of the company in 2007. For 2011 it was decided he should have a slightly different look for advertising campaigns and the website.

Following an advertising campaign entitled “Power up your website!” A superman version of the character was born. This is not the first time that the character has been dressed up, I’ve been asked to redraw him in a variety of poses.
